Signals

The table below defines the actual physical connections of the CRD signals to the data acquisition cards. The headers are defined as below:

  • Signal - description of the signal
  • Voltage - voltage range of the signal
  • Resolution - resolution of the signal in bits (where applicable)
  • Rate - acquisition/generation frequency
  • Device - physical NI DAQ card that the signal is connected to
  • Channel - DAQ defined channel of the signal; this value is used to identify the connection point in software
  • Connector Pins - the physical connection point of the signal to the card itself
  • Position in Task - position of the signal in the DAQmx task relative to the other signals
  • notes - pertinent notes pertaining to the signal

All signals for the CRDS not shared with other instruments are routed through one of two cards - an S-series which has the capability of acquiring 8 channels of data at 2.5 MHz/ch simultaneously and an M-series that is used for acquiring lower frequency (housekeeping data).
